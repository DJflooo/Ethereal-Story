Chapter 1: The Awakening




As the sun was waking up, barely piercing heavy and grey clouds, a man was laying flat, his face in the sand. After a few minutes, his head went up, his eyes half-closed from the bursts of light around him. He was wearing rags, dirty and with sand all over them. As he stood up, still a bit wobbly, he asked himself: *Where am I?* But he quickly found a better question:

*Wait, who am I? I can't... remember anything... How do I even know I don't know?* Feeling the headache coming from a mile away, he quickly stopped thinking about it and instead looked around him. The rain was pouring on him, lightning striking the trees and the ground indiscriminately, the sun only slightly visible through the grey clouds. Then, he heard some walking not to far away. As he began walking towards there, he took some fruits from the ground. Not the prettiest ones, but better than nothing.

As he went up the hill, he saw a man standing just besides a simple grave. The man had mildly short black hair, and was only wearing rags too. The black haired man started talking as soon as he turned around, hearing the footsteps:

"You're awake... I thought you'd be out for a few more days."

"Who are you?"

"Huh, are you serious? The two of us barely got out of there alive, and now you have memory loss... I can't believe it..." After a short pause, the man standing at the side of the grave continued. "I'm going to assume you don't remember even our names. I'm Morden, and you're Lucan Ether."

*Lucan Ether* thought Lucan, trying to remember it, to see if it really fit him. He said it to himself, but nothing really felt like 'home'. "What happened to us?"

"We were being held captive in a remote location," continued Morden, "and they performed magic experiments on us. We saw people die every day... but I guess you don't remember that. That's the reason of this grave too. Tucker was his name."

"I'm so-"

"It's not your fault... we did everything we could. It's a shame that Tucker couldn't make it, but at least he's had a proper burial." Morden cut in. "Anyway, we're free now. You still remember how to use magic, right? Since we're magic-users, we'll make it far in this world, we just need to meet the right people."

Just as Morden mentioned magic, Lucan felt something twist inside him, like it just... woke up? Morden still had some things to say, postponing his thoughts on this feeling:

"You can take one of the rowboats and go wherever you want."

"And what are you going to do?"

"I'm going to stay here for a bit... I need to... to think some things over."

"Where should I go?"

"I don't know... you're hungry aren't you? Maybe you should go to Redwake, it's a port to the east of here. They might have food for you, and being there may help you to remember some things from your past as well." As Lucan was going back to the beach, Morden added: "Maybe we'll see each other again some day, Lucan. Until then, farewell, and I wish you good fortune."

At the rowboat, Lucan found two... pieces of wood? A strange circular object with two triangles? He... didn't really understand what they were. Though, with the little forks on both sides of the rowboat, he guessed that the two wood pieces would fit there.

But before trying anything, he wanted to focus on this.. thing... in his body. It was like some kind of... vapor. He simply thought about pushing it out of his hands, and out came a... kind of light? It wasn't pure light, but more... Hot? Like if it was some kind of red "fire-light". For now he still didn't know much, but seeing the small orb of "fire-light" floating above his hand was pretty cool.

And then he finally remembered he actually needed to get in the rowboat.



Lucan went in the rowboat, and suddently, muscle-memory kicked in. He took the two pieces of wood - that now he remembered were called oars - and put them in the 'forks' - or oarlocks. He sat down in the middle and started moving the oars in a circular motion... the wrong way. After he realized he wasn't going anywhere, he changed the direction of his movements, and finally sailed off of the tiny island. He then grabbed the other object - the circular one - and looked at it more thoroughly. It had four letters: N, S, W, E. He thought for a bit.

*Morden asked me to go to the East. And I don't have any way to know where I go... So this must be what I need to use! And because East starts with an E, it should be there! But which of the triangles do I need to follow... Red or white?"

He finally decided on the white one, out of pure luck. As such, he started rowing in this direction. The waves were gentle, slightly pushing his rowboat upwards each time one passed. Well... until one that nearly made him lose the circular object. He could almost taste the sea's salt on his lips while he continued to think about himself.

*Morden talked about experiments, and people doing them on us. So I've been experimented on? Why? What did they do? And who are they? Maybe I should try to find the answers to those ques-*

Lucan's train of thought was cut short by the sighting of a big, dark figure under the sea, getting bigger and bigger, until finally, it jumped out of the water. The massive red shark was already looking towards Lucan's rowboat with its glowing, white eyes. It angled itself towards Lucan and started falling towards him.

Lucan rowed furiously to dodge the shark, barely avoiding it, the waves created almost sending him out of the tiny boat.

However, the monster wasn't finished yet. It got back to the surface and quickly swam towards him, opening its jaws, one above it and one below the ship. But before it could close them...

*Shlack!*

A harpoon pierced the side of the shark. Then a second, a third, a fourth... More than a dozen pierced its flanks while it slowly turned towards the attackers. But before it could completely turn around, a gigantic spear of fire emerged from a... weird and complicated circle, and went through the head of the behemoth, killing it on the spot.

As the shark was slowly sinking into the depths of the ocean, Lucan saw the ship behind it that saved him. It was quite a bit bigger that his, with two sails. The ship in itself was purple and yellow, and the sails beared a strange symbol: a... bulb... that seemed to emit light, with its outlines purple.

The people on it were too concerned about reeling the big shark to even notice him. His curiosity got the better of him, making him row towards the boat full of strangers. He got closer to the side opposite to the one where people were reeling, while still remaining behind the corpse.

"Well, I never thought we would find a White Eyes here," said a woman voice.

"I found it, not 'we'," said another one, playfully.

"Yeah yeah, 'sorry, oh senser of shark'! Remind me who launched the final attack?"

"I'm feeling like I'm getting attacked verbally..."

"And you are."

"Hey! Can't you come help us?" said a male voice on the other side.

"Nah, too lazy."

"Same."

"Really?!" said the man with indignation.

"Hey, I'm your superior, I do what I want."

"Well the superio' bette' get her ass ove' he'e before I tell the higha'-ups." said another man, approaching.

"The higher-ups don't care about you."

"Are ya talkin' badly of the Lightbringers?!" said the man with false indignation.

"Oh no! What will I do? I should kill myself just for this!"

"... Wait, guys, I'm sensing something near the ship..." said the other woman.

"Ya sure it's not the crew Temia?" asked the man.

"Of course I am. I also know it's not White Eyes, its dead. And it's not like a fish-"

"WAIT! Maybe it's an arcane fish!" exclaimed the man, while running could be heard.

"Tssk, of course it's not. Riley, you sense it too?" asked Temia.

"I just did... It seems humanoid. Wait, I'm seeing the tip of a boat behind the White Eyes."

"Really- oh yeah, I just saw it! HEY! IF YOU CAN HEAR US, SHOW YOURSELF!"

"You know, you don't need to say 'if you can hear us', because if they can't, they won't hear you say it..." half-whispered Riley.

Lucan slowly went out of his cover, which was still getting reeled by the crew. He could finally see the people that were talking: a tall white woman with a purple sweater, yellow armbands and brown pants, and the other, a slightly more tanned woman, wore a slightly dimmer yellow tank top and dark-purple shorts. They both had a small badge with the same logo as the one on the sails near their shoulders. Close to them was a man wearing a full purple-ish armor, with only his head visible. He put his hand on the hilt of his sword when he saw Lucan.

"Hey there!" said Riley, smiling. "What are you doing around here?"

"And who are you?" asked Temia before Lucan could say anything.

"I... uh, I'm Lucan. I'm trying to get to Redwake. I was told it was East from where I was..."

"Then you are going the right wa-" Temia couldn't finish her sentence before Riley punched the top of her head.

"It's the opposite way." clarified Riley, Temia getting back up.

"Oh, thanks. I wasn't really sure on how to use this." said Lucan, showing his compass.

"Well, the red arrow shows where you are goi-"

"WHERE IS DA FISH?!" said the other man that ran when an arcane fish was mentioned. He was a bit more... 'voluptuous' than the others.

"There are NO FISHES HERE!" answered Riley, giving away another punch. "Ugh, sorry about that. So yeah, the red arrow is where you're pointing at, while the white one is the opposite."

"How do you not know how to use a compass anyway?" asked Temia, having regained her composure.

"Well... I just woke up, like, a few minutes ago, and I can't remember anything from before."

"Hm, I see. I still sense magic in you though." said Temia.

"Yeah, I can do this." answered Lucan, a small orb of his fire-light above his hand. "I don't know what it is though..."

"Hm..."

"Yeah, not sure either..."

"So, really no fish here?" asked the man that just got back up.

"NO, THERE ARE NO FISHES HERE, GRAMIH! Now go help the rest of the crew!" said Riley, annoyed.

"Yeah, I don't recognize this one," said Temia, turning toward Riley, while Gramih was walking towards the rest of the crew, a sad look on his face. "Do you?"

"Nah, me neither. Bert?" she asked the armored man that stayed silent all throughout the conversation, despite the action.

"It's Plasma magic. Not that rare, but not that common either," he said, no emotion in his voice.

*Plasma? What even is that?*

"Oooh, plasma? Interesting." Riley looked back to her crew, then back to Lucan. "I think we're almost finished, and with your little rowboat, you shouldn't stay too long out in the sea. Go to Redwake, they should have somewhere for you to sleep in. And if you see anyone like us, you can be sure they won't attack you."

"And who are you really?" asked Lucan.

"We are part of the Lightbringer's Fleet. And Riley here," said Temia, pointing at her, "is one of the Vice-Captains of the Fleet. And an Amber Lantern, which is pretty high rank."

"While she only is a Torch-"

"I'm gonna blow you out of this carave-"

And the two of them ran off, leaving only Bert with Lucan. The former talked first.

"Good luck to you. It must be pretty hard to live with memory loss. Just know the Lightbringers are with you. For now," he said the last part almost like a warning, while Lucan was sitting back in position to row, now reading his compass in the right way.

"Yeah, good luck to you too. Especially with those two."

The last words almost made Bert smile while he shook his head, the two girls fighting in the middle of the ship, the whole crew watching them instead of reeling the White Eyes.

And Lucan started rowing again.



End of Chapter 1: The Awakening
